Chelsea Post-War Apartment Renovation

555 W 23rd St, NYC, NY:

Uptown elegance meets downtown charm

This apartment renovation took place in the trendy neighborhood of Chelsea, located in a luxury doorman condominium constructed in 2006. Chelsea is a neighborhood known for its art galleries, high end skyscrapers, fashion, and diverse food scene, making it an ideal location if you seek a blend of uptown elegance and downtown charm. In this 1-bedroom apartment, our client goal was to modernize the overall look throughout the apartment, thus, we replaced the outdated dark hardwood flooring with brand new white oak flooring, which filled the entire apartment with a sense of luminosity and spaciousness. In the kitchen, the Chapter team replaced the countertops with a stunning Caesarstone white snow quartz and crafted a counter seating area for the peninsula, which not only added functional utility but also an air of sophistication. In addition, we installed a new light-colored backsplash, which made the existing cabinets pop more and create contrast in the entire kitchen. Lastly, we replaced the lighting fixtures and installed beautiful pendants that will catch the eye of anyone walking into the kitchen.

For the bedroom, the client desired a larger closet, so we meticulously built out the existing one, transforming it into a semi-walk-in closet, complete with multiple shelves and rods, providing more storage and organization. The client wanted the bathroom to appear brighter. Therefore, we replaced the existing tiles and strategically installed an elegant accent tile on the rear wall, which contrasted with the plumbing fixtures, creating a visually striking aesthetic. In addition, we installed a beautiful custom-made vanity with three mirror medicine cabinets above. Lastly, we installed a glass panel on top of the bathtub for convenience, which also gives the entire room a chique finish.
