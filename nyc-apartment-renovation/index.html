Apartment renovation in NYC: what’s involved

Renovating an apartment in NYC requires detailed planning, coordination, and navigating building regulations. Chapter takes full responsibility for managing your apartment renovation, ensuring every aspect aligns with your vision, budget, and the requirements of your building.

Here are the key components involved in an NYC apartment renovation:

Here's a broad outline of the usual components involved in a full home renovation:

Planning & design

We work with you to define your renovation goals. Our complimentary design services help create a layout that maximizes space and budget, turning your vision into reality.

Architecture & building approval

We prepares a full architectural package to meet NYC codes and building requirements. We handle the building board approval process, securing permits and co-op or condo board approvals to prevent delays.

Demolition & site protection

We carefully protect common areas and neighboring units during demolition. Old walls, flooring, and fixtures are removed while minimizing disruption and preventing damage.

Bathroom renovation

Our bathroom renovations focus on maximizing space and comfort. We install new fixtures, tiles, and waterproofing systems while optimizing layouts for functionality and aesthetic appeal.

Kitchen renovation

Our kitchen renovations balance functionality and style. We revamp layouts, upgrade cabinetry, appliances, countertops, and backsplashes, creating a beautiful, efficient cooking space.

Electrical & plumbing upgrades

We update electrical and plumbing systems, installing new lines, fixtures, and appliances to meet modern standards. All work complies with NYC building codes, ensuring functionality and efficiency for your renovated space.

HVAC systems

We evaluate and update your apartment’s heating, ventilation, and air conditioning systems, maximizing comfort and energy efficiency according to your space’s needs.

Custom carpentry & millwork

Construct, repair, and install frameworks and structures, including framing, drywall, structural modifications, doors, windows, trim, and other elements.

Quality assurance & final inspections

Once construction is complete, we close out permits and conduct a thorough quality assurance process. We ensure your renovation meets all NYC building codes and delivers on your expectations.

FAQs

The cost of an apartment renovation in NYC can vary significantly depending on the scope of the project, the size of the apartment, and the finishes selected. On average, a full renovation starts at $150 per sqft, depending on these factors. Chapter works closely with you to maximize your budget and ensure that every dollar is spent wisely.

An alteration agreement is a legally binding document between you and your building's management that outlines the approved scope of work for your renovation project. It acts as a safeguard for both parties, ensuring your project complies with building rules and minimizes disruptions to other residents. Failing to adhere to this agreement can lead to penalties, project halts, or even legal action.

Approval timelines vary greatly depending on the building and the complexity of the project. Simpler renovations may only take a few weeks, while extensive projects like full gut renovations can take several months. It's crucial to factor in these timelines during your project planning.

Yes, every building has unique restrictions outlined in its alteration agreement. These might include limitations on materials used, allowable design elements, and acceptable construction methods. Familiarize yourself with these restrictions before finalizing your renovation plans.

While specific requirements might vary, most NYC buildings mandate soundproofing measures to ensure privacy and minimize noise transfer between units. This typically involves incorporating soundproofing materials into walls, floors, and ceilings during the renovation process.

The "Wet Over Dry" rule is a building code guideline stating that wet areas like kitchens and bathrooms should not be located directly above dry living spaces in units below. This rule prevents water damage and ensures structural integrity, impacting the layout options for your renovation.

While both require alteration agreements, co-op boards are generally stricter and may require more detailed plans, interviews, and have more limitations on the scope of work. Condos typically have a more lenient approval process and offer greater flexibility in renovation choices. This difference stems from the ownership structure, where co-op residents own shares in the building rather than their unit directly.
